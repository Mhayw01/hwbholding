Our services & benefits

Move between both saunas, the cold plunge baths and the wellbeing room — it’s all included.

Cold · 6–10°C
Contrast therapy

Cold Plunge Baths

Two deep ice plunge baths kept between 6–10°C for powerful contrast therapy.

What it does: Cold immersion stimulates blood flow, reduces inflammation, and releases endorphins. Whether you’re an athlete, shift worker, or just need a reset, you’ll feel the mental clarity and physical relief almost instantly.

Why it works: Used regularly, cold therapy can support better sleep, a stronger immune system, and reduced muscle soreness.

Step in, reset your system, and leave feeling recharged.

Heat
High dry heat

Traditional Finnish Sauna

A high-temperature, dry-air sauna with centuries of healing heritage.

What it does: The intense heat promotes full-body detox, supports cardiovascular health, improves breathing, and relaxes deep muscle tension. As your body sweats, it flushes out toxins, clears the skin, and eases tightness.

Why it works: The dry air raises your core temperature, increasing circulation, calming the nervous system, and triggering natural recovery from the inside out.

Sweat it out, breathe deep, come back to balance.

Infrared
Deep, gentle heat

Infrared Sauna

A dry heat therapy that uses infrared light to gently warm the body from within, not just the air around you.

What it does: Infrared waves penetrate deep into muscles and joints, easing pain, improving circulation, boosting cell regeneration and promoting better sleep. Ideal after training, during recovery, or in times of stress.

Why it works: Unlike traditional saunas, infrared heat reaches deeper layers of tissue, raising your internal temperature and supporting natural detox, inflammation reduction, and hormonal balance.

Let the warmth sink in, and feel your body start to heal.

Recovery
Compression recovery

Presso-therapy

Dynamic air compression boots and arm sleeves that massage your legs and arms, flushing out fatigue after training, a shift on your feet, or a long day.

What it does: Sequential pressure waves move through each chamber, boosting circulation, clearing out lactic acid and easing swelling, so tired, heavy arms and legs feel lighter and properly recovered.

Why it works: By gently pumping blood and lymph back toward the heart, presso-therapy speeds up your body’s natural recovery, reduces muscle soreness and supports better movement, whether you train hard or are simply on your feet all day.

Sit back, let the pressure do the work, and feel your arms and legs come back to life.

Calm
Your sanctuary

Community Well-being Room

A calming space to pause, reflect, and connect — whether you stretch, meditate, breathe or simply sit still.

It’s designed to be yours: a moment of quiet before the cold, a place to write or recharge after the sauna. The room adapts to group sessions, 1-to-1 wellbeing work, or solo reflection, always supporting your mental and emotional reset.

Your calm space to rest, reflect, or simply be.

Community garden
Outdoor safe space

Community Garden

A quiet green space in the heart of Barry, built by the community for the community, where you can step away from the noise and simply breathe.

Created through community collaboration and the support of local businesses, the garden gives everyone a calm outdoor place to relax, reflect and connect with others. Share a brew, get your hands in the soil, or just sit a while in the fresh air.

Time outdoors, alongside people who understand, is one of the simplest ways to lift your mood and feel less alone. It is open to veterans, families and the whole community, with no pressure and no judgment.

A safe space to slow down, get some air, and feel part of something.

Our story, Jake Hudson

From Royal Navy to community champion

Our Story, Jake Hudson

At 16, Jake joined the Royal Navy, where he learned discipline, leadership and the value of camaraderie. Life changed when he had to return home unexpectedly following the loss of his mum.

Years later, his biggest battle came after leaving the service, when he faced significant mental health challenges and some of the darkest periods of his life.

Throughout it all, one person never gave up on him, his wife, Kim.

When Jake struggled to believe in himself, Kim believed in him enough for both of them. Through every setback, every difficult day and every challenge, she remained his rock, his strength and the person who kept him moving forward.

Alongside their children, her unwavering love, patience and support became the foundation of his recovery and helped him rediscover purpose, hope and a belief that better days were ahead.

That journey would eventually inspire the creation of Health Hwb.

We only went for a sauna

Where it began

We Only Went For A Sauna

Sometimes the smallest acts of kindness can change a life. At a time when Jake was struggling with his mental health and finding it difficult to see a way forward, Helen and Ian George saw something he couldn’t see in himself. Potential. Not the struggles he was facing, but the person they knew he could become. Rather than walking away, they reached out.

They invited him to West Wales for the day to experience contrast therapy. A sauna. An ice bath. A chance to step away from the noise and simply breathe. What started as a simple invitation became much more than that.

Their kindness, encouragement and unwavering belief helped Jake begin to see a future beyond the challenges he was facing. They became trusted friends, mentors and a constant source of support, helping him realise that no matter how difficult life becomes, there is always a way forward. More importantly, they helped him believe in himself again.

More than anything, Helen and Ian simply wanted to see Jake, Kim and the boys come through the difficult times and build the life they deserved. They saw a family worth believing in and never stopped encouraging them forward, even when the road ahead seemed uncertain. Their support was never about recognition or reward. It came from a place of genuine care and a desire to see others succeed.

The phrase “We Only Went For A Sauna” captures the moment everything began. A reminder that sometimes all it takes is one act of kindness, one conversation, and people willing to believe in you when you can’t yet believe in yourself.

What started with a sauna became a vision, and what started as an act of kindness became Health Hwb. The rest, as they say, is history.

Step into Jade Yoga and the pace of the day softens. This is Jade’s space within the Hwb, a calm room of plants, warm light and quiet intention, built for wellness and therapies that bring body, mind and soul back into harmony.

Naturopathy & nutritional therapy

Testament Health & Wellness

Rooted in nature, guided by science. Testament Health and Wellness brings naturopathy, nutritional therapy and functional medicine to the Hwb, getting to the root cause of ongoing health concerns rather than just treating the symptoms.

Founded and led by practitioner Tess, it takes a holistic, research-backed approach tailored to you. From low energy, poor sleep and brain fog to digestive issues and chronic or autoimmune conditions, the focus is on supporting your body’s natural ability to heal, regain balance and thrive.

Services include nutritional therapy, herbal medicine, mycotherapy, micro-immunotherapy, IV and IM nutrient therapy, advanced testing and phlebotomy. It all starts with a free discovery call to talk through your goals and build a personalised plan.

Moving for mind, body & recovery

Vale Runners

We’re proud to be collaborating with Vale Runners, a free, all-abilities running group based right here in Barry, and proudly voted Run Wales Running Group of the Year 2022.

More than a running club, they’re a community built on encouragement, inclusivity and the joy of movement. With weekly sessions ranging from 3K to 10K, hill sprints to easy-paced chatty runs, they create space for everyone, from complete beginners to seasoned runners, to move their body, clear their mind and connect with others.

The support doesn’t stop at the finish line. Vale Runners use the Hwb’s contrast therapy facilities, combining heat and cold exposure as a powerful recovery tool, whether it’s easing tired muscles after a long run or building mental resilience.
